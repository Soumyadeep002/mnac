ABOUT
OUR MISSION

We are committed to safeguarding the planet by restoring nature and cleansing the environment of man-made pollutants. Through a scientific approach and innovative solutions, we aim to create a greener, more sustainable world.

Our mission extends beyond conservation—we strive to inspire a spirit of adventure, explore uncharted territories, and lead impactful environmental movements. By educating and empowering children and youth across the country, we cultivate a deep connection to nature.

Moreover, we stand ready to serve communities in times of distress and natural calamities, making a positive difference where it matters most.

Nature Study

The MAKAUT Nature and Adventure Club (MNAC) offers hands-on experiences in environmental exploration and conservation through its immersive Nature Study camps. From the scenic landscapes of Susunia Hills in 2022 to other breathtaking locations, these camps ignite a passion for nature in participants. Activities like trekking, rock climbing, and environmental education take place in picturesque settings, blending adventure with learning. Through these programs, participants cultivate a deep awareness of sustainability, biodiversity, and environmental stewardship. Our goal is to inspire individuals to take an active role in preserving our planet while fostering a spirit of adventure and connection with nature.

Rock Climbing

Rock climbing is an exhilarating sport that challenges both your physical and mental limits. Using only your hands, feet, and specialized gear like ropes, harnesses, and carabiners, participants navigate natural rock formations. Each climb offers a unique experience, with routes varying in difficulty, height, and terrain. From multi-pitch climbs that require advanced techniques to bouldering's short, rope-free ascents, the thrill of climbing is unmatched. Beyond boosting physical fitness, rock climbing sharpens problem-solving, builds self-confidence, and promotes teamwork. It’s also a powerful way to connect with nature and foster environmental awareness—just like the 2024 MNAC Rock Climbing Course at Gajaburu Hills, where adventure meets sustainability.​
